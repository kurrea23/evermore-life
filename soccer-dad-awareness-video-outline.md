# "Soccer Dad" — Evermore Life Awareness Video Outline

**Campaign:** First Evermore Life brand campaign
**Platform:** Facebook (feed + Reels-eligible cuts)
**Goal:** Awareness — introduce Evermore Life as the company that protects everyday families
**Format:** 6 scenes, ~1 minute each (~6 min hero film). Pull 15s / 30s / 60s cutdowns from this for paid + feed.
**Tone:** Warm, real, unscripted-feeling. Not a sales pitch — a slice of life that lands an emotional truth.
**Core idea:** The same dad who shows up to every Saturday game is the one quietly making sure his family is okay no matter what. Evermore is how he does the second part.

---

## Scene 1 — "Saturday Morning" (~1:00)
**Visual:** Early light. Alarm goes off. Dad (40s, ordinary, likable) quietly gets up before everyone. Pours cereal, fills water bottles, finds the missing shin guard under the couch. Kid stumbles out half-asleep in a soccer jersey.
**Audio:** Natural sound — coffee, zippers, a dog. Soft acoustic bed underneath.
**VO (dad, warm, reflective):** "Nobody tells you that being a dad is mostly logistics. Cleats, snacks, who's got the orange slices this week."
**On-screen text:** *(none yet — let it breathe)*
**Purpose:** Establish the relatable everyman. No brand yet. Earn attention.

## Scene 2 — "Game Day" (~1:00)
**Visual:** The field. Dad on the sideline with a folding chair, cheering, filming on his phone, high-fiving other parents. Kid scores (or just tries hard and falls). Pure joy. Slow-mo of the celebration.
**Audio:** Cheering, whistle, laughter. Music lifts.
**VO:** "This is the part everybody sees. The cheering, the carpool, the guy who never misses a game."
**On-screen text:** *"He shows up. Every time."*
**Purpose:** Peak warmth. Build the emotional high before the turn.

## Scene 3 — "The Quiet Part" (~1:00)
**Visual:** Tone shifts — gentler, more intimate. That night, house quiet, kids asleep. Dad at the kitchen table with a laptop. Not stressed, just thoughtful. Looks down the hallway at the kids' rooms.
**Audio:** Music softens to a single piano/guitar line. Less ambient noise.
**VO:** "But there's another part nobody sees. The part where you think about the games you might not be there for. And you make sure they're covered anyway."
**On-screen text:** *"Showing up isn't just Saturdays."*
**Purpose:** The turn. Introduce the real subject — protecting your family's future — without fear-mongering.

## Scene 4 — "Why Evermore" (~1:00)
**Visual:** Clean, simple. Dad on the couch on his phone/laptop, getting a quote — it's genuinely easy, a few taps. Intercut with quick warm flashes: a younger kid, a partner, a paid-off moment of relief. Show the brand name on screen for the first time.
**Audio:** Music steady and hopeful.
**VO:** "Evermore Life is built for people like him. Real coverage, honest pricing, no stuffy insurance runaround. Just protection that fits an actual family's life."
**On-screen text:** *"Life insurance made simple. Evermore Life."*
**Purpose:** Brand reveal + the promise. Keep it human, not corporate.

## Scene 5 — "What It's Really About" (~1:00)
**Visual:** Montage of everyday "showing up" moments across different families (diverse — single moms, grandparents, young couples) — not just our soccer dad. Bandaging a knee, teaching to ride a bike, packing a lunch, a first day of school.
**Audio:** Music swells to its fullest, warm and uplifting.
**VO:** "Because protecting the people you love isn't about insurance. It's about making sure that whatever happens, they're still okay."
**On-screen text:** *"For every family that shows up."*
**Purpose:** Widen from one dad to the whole audience. This is the shareable, emotional core.

## Scene 6 — "Close" (~1:00)
**Visual:** Back to the soccer field, golden hour. Dad and kid walking to the car, cleats over the shoulder, easy conversation. Pull back wide. Logo resolves over the field.
**Audio:** Music settles to a satisfying resolve.
**VO:** "Evermore Life. Be there for them — no matter what."
**On-screen text / end card:**
- *Evermore Life* (logo)
- *Get a free quote in minutes — evermorelife.com*
- *[CTA button overlay for Facebook: "Learn More"]*
**Purpose:** Clear, soft call to action. Awareness first, low-pressure next step.

---

## Production notes
- **Casting:** Real-feeling, not model-perfect. The relatability is the whole campaign.
- **Music:** One continuous track that builds — license one bed and let it carry all six scenes for cohesion.
- **Captions:** Burn in subtitles. ~85% of Facebook video is watched on mute — the on-screen text lines above should carry the story without sound.
- **Aspect ratios:** Shoot 4K so you can deliver 1:1 (feed), 9:16 (Reels/Stories), and 16:9 (YouTube/landing page) from the same footage.
- **Diversity:** Scene 5 is where you show this is for everyone, not just one demographic.

## Cutdowns for paid (pull from the hero film)
- **60s** — Scenes 1, 3, 4, 6 (the emotional spine + CTA).
- **30s** — Scene 2 high → Scene 3 turn → Scene 4 brand → end card.
- **15s (Reels/feed hook)** — Open on the Scene 2 celebration, cut to "Showing up isn't just Saturdays," hard cut to logo + CTA.

## Compliance reminder
Life insurance ad copy is regulated. Before publishing, confirm any claims about coverage, pricing, or guarantees with your licensing/compliance requirements for the states you operate in (AZ, TX, and pending AR), and include any required disclosures on the end card or in the post copy.
