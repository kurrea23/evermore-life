# 02 — Ideal Client Map

## Ideal Client 1: The Family Protector

### Profile

- Age: 30–55.
- Has children, spouse, mortgage, or dependents.
- May be healthy enough for term or permanent coverage.
- Wants to protect income, mortgage, children, spouse, or long-term family stability.
- May not understand the difference between term, whole life, and IUL.

### What They Want

- To know their family will be okay.
- Affordable monthly coverage.
- A simple explanation without pressure.
- Confidence that they are not overpaying.
- A trusted guide who compares options.

### What They Fear

- Dying before their family is financially stable.
- Leaving a spouse with bills.
- Their children losing opportunities.
- Being sold the wrong policy.
- Overcommitting to something they cannot afford.

### Best Story Angles

- Child growing up.
- Mortgage/family home protection.
- Parent sacrificing for their child’s future.
- “What happens if your income stops?”
- “Protection while your children still need you most.”

### Best Product Conversation

Start broad:

> “Depending on your age, health, budget, and goals, we may look at term, whole life, final expense, IUL, or another type of coverage. The goal is not to force one product. The goal is to match the right coverage to the person.”

## Ideal Client 2: The Final Expense Planner

### Profile

- Age: 50–80.
- Often has grown children or grandchildren.
- May have health concerns.
- Wants to avoid leaving funeral costs or final bills behind.
- May want a smaller whole-life/final-expense policy.

### What They Want

- Simple approval process.
- Predictable monthly payment.
- Clear explanation.
- A policy that does not expire if premiums are paid.
- Peace of mind for children/family.

### What They Fear

- Funeral/burial costs.
- Medical bills or final expenses.
- Being denied because of health.
- Being confused by insurance language.
- Family conflict after passing.

### Best Story Angles

- Adult child remembering their parent.
- Tree/acorn/generational legacy.
- “Give your family clarity.”
- “Your love deserves a plan.”
- “Final expenses should not become final confusion.”

### Best Product Conversation

Start with dignity:

> “Final expense coverage is usually about creating a clear plan for the people you love, so they are not trying to figure everything out during an already emotional time.”

## Audience Segments for Content

### Cold Organic Audience

They do not know Evermore Life yet.

Best content:
- Emotional story shorts.
- Legacy reels.
- Parent/child cinematic videos.
- Simple “what is life insurance?” clips.

### Warm Audience

They watched 50–95% of videos or visited the page.

Best content:
- Educational clips.
- Explainers.
- “How we help compare options.”
- “Term vs final expense vs permanent coverage.”

### Hot Audience

They submitted a form, opened SMS/email, or chatted with Sarah/AI.

Best content:
- Appointment reminders.
- FAQ.
- Trust/reassurance.
- “What to expect on the call.”
- “What info we need to compare options.”

## Messaging Ladder

1. **Emotion:** “Your legacy moves on.”
2. **Reflection:** “Have you protected the people who carry your story?”
3. **Education:** “Coverage depends on age, health, budget, and goals.”
4. **Trust:** “We compare options and help match coverage to your needs.”
5. **Action:** “Request a simple coverage review.”
