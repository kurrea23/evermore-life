# 01 — Narrative Bible

## Big Idea

Evermore Life is not selling “a policy.”  
Evermore Life is helping people protect the people who still carry their love, work, and sacrifice forward.

## Central Story Engine

Every story follows this arc:

1. **A parent has a dream.**
2. **Life requires sacrifice.**
3. **The parent pours that dream/love into their child.**
4. **The child grows, wins, becomes, or continues the family story.**
5. **The parent realizes legacy is not what they achieved alone — it is what keeps living through the people they love.**
6. **Evermore Life enters as the practical act of protecting that legacy.**

## Emotional Formula

```text
Love + Time + Sacrifice + Continuation = Legacy
Legacy + Responsibility = Life Insurance
```

## Main Symbol

### The Evermore Tree

The tree represents:

- Family roots.
- Protection.
- Growth over time.
- A parent’s sacrifice.
- A child carrying the story forward.
- A life that continues through love and memory.

### Acorn Motif

The acorn represents:

- A child.
- A new beginning.
- A small act that grows over decades.
- A parent planting something they may not personally see fully mature.

## Brand Voice

Warm, grounded, sincere, human, protective.

Avoid language that feels manipulative, predatory, or overly fear-based.

## Core Lines

Use these across videos, landing pages, captions, and retargeting ads.

- “Your legacy moves on.”
- “Be there evermore.”
- “The love you gave them keeps growing.”
- “Protection is one more way to show up.”
- “Life insurance is not about leaving. It is about still being there.”
- “You spent your life protecting them. Let’s protect what comes next.”
- “The people you love deserve clarity, not confusion.”

## Story Types

### 1. The Soccer Son Story

A father once loved soccer. Life shifted. He worked hard, chose family, and watched his son naturally inherit the same fire. The father supports him through practice, sacrifice, and quiet effort. Years later, the son wins on a major stage and runs into the crowd to hug his father first.

**Closing line:**  
“Some dreams do not end. They move through the people we love.”

### 2. The Chef Daughter Story

A parent teaches their child to cook after long workdays. Years later, the child becomes a chef and invites the parent to the restaurant opening.

**Closing line:**  
“The meals became memories. The memories became a life.”

### 3. The Graduation Story

A single parent works extra shifts so their child can go to school. Years later, the child walks across a graduation stage and looks for the parent in the crowd.

**Closing line:**  
“Every late night had a reason.”

### 4. The Workshop Story

A grandfather teaches a young child how to fix things. Years later, the child opens a business and keeps the same old tool on the wall.

**Closing line:**  
“What we teach with our hands can live for generations.”

### 5. The Wedding Story

A parent helps a child through heartbreak, growth, and adulthood. At the wedding, the child squeezes their parent’s hand before walking forward.

**Closing line:**  
“Being there is the gift they remember.”

## The Offer Bridge

The story should transition into Evermore Life like this:

```text
You showed up for them in life.
Life insurance is one more way to show up — with clarity, protection, and peace of mind.
```

## Never Say

Avoid these unless legally reviewed:

- “Guaranteed approval” unless the product truly is guaranteed issue and all limitations are disclosed.
- “Everyone qualifies.”
- “Free money.”
- “No medical questions” unless accurate for the specific product.
- “You will not be a burden.”
- “Your family will suffer if you do not buy this.”
- Exact price claims without age, health, state, carrier, and product context.
