# Additional Legacy Story Scripts
## Evermore Life — Alternate Story Arcs for Content Calendar

**Source:** Expanded from Cinematic Legacy Campaign (01_Narrative_Bible.md + 05_Short_Form_Storyboards.md)
**Use:** Month 2–3 content calendar. These run after the Soccer Dad hero video establishes the brand universe.

---

## Universal Story Arc (Apply to All)

Every Evermore Life legacy story follows this structure:

1. A parent has a dream (or a skill, or a gift)
2. Life requires sacrifice — the parent puts their child first
3. The parent pours love into the child (teaching, showing up, supporting)
4. The child grows, wins, becomes — because of what the parent planted
5. The parent realizes: legacy is not what they achieved alone, but what keeps living through love
6. Evermore Life enters as the practical act of protecting that legacy

## Story 2 — The Chef's Kitchen

**Format:** Short form 35–45 seconds / can extend to 2-min medium form
**ICP:** Both (universal parent/child emotion)

### Shot List
1. A small kitchen — simple, worn, loved. Evening.
2. A parent's hands kneading dough while a small daughter watches from a stool.
3. Flour on both their faces. Laughter.
4. The parent working a late shift — tired, still in uniform.
5. The daughter, now a teenager, alone in the kitchen — recreating the recipe from memory.
6. Flash forward: a restaurant kitchen. Clean, professional. She is the chef now.
7. The restaurant opens. She stands at the door.
8. The parent walks in — the first guest.
9. They hug near the kitchen doorway.
10. Evermore tree logo close.

### Voiceover
```
It started as dinner after a long day.
A small kitchen.
A tired parent.
A child who just wanted to help.

But love has a way of becoming direction.

She watched. She learned. She carried it forward —
not because anyone told her to,
but because some things pass through the hands.

Years later, she opened her own kitchen.
And the first meal she served was for the person who taught her.

The little things you do become the roots they grow from.
```

### On-Screen Text
*"The love you gave them keeps growing."*
*Evermore Life — Be There Evermore*

## Story 3 — The Graduation Night

**Format:** Short form 30–45 seconds
**ICP:** ICP1 (Protective Parent) — single parent angle is powerful

### Shot List
1. A single parent leaving before sunrise — uniform, lunch packed, dark outside.
2. Child asleep, schoolbooks open on the pillow.
3. Parent at the kitchen table late — bills, calculator, college application forms.
4. Child studying. Flash of struggle, late nights, determination.
5. Graduation ceremony. A name is called.
6. The child walks the stage — eyes scanning the crowd.
7. They find their parent. The parent is standing, hand over heart.
8. They run to each other after.
9. Tree roots visual.

### Voiceover
```
They may not remember every late night.
But they will remember that you never gave up.

Every early morning.
Every second job.
Every quiet moment when you chose their future over your comfort.

And then one day — they walk across a stage.
And every hard day finally has a face.

That is legacy.
That is what love actually looks like.
```

### On-Screen Text
*"Every late night had a reason."*

## Story 4 — The Workshop

**Format:** Short form 30–40 seconds
**ICP:** ICP2 (Adult Child) — intergenerational, grandfather angle

### Shot List
1. An old workshop — garage or basement. Wood shavings on the floor, tools on pegboard.
2. A grandfather teaching a young child how to hold a hammer properly. Patient.
3. Their hands together on the handle.
4. Close-up of a simple birdhouse being built together.
5. Flash forward: the child, now an adult, in their own workspace — a business.
6. On the wall behind them: the original old birdhouse. Kept all these years.
7. The adult touches it briefly as they pass. A small, private moment.
8. Tree/acorn logo.

### Voiceover
```
He didn't talk much.
He taught with his hands.

Every Saturday, they went to the workshop.
And slowly — the child learned that broken things can be fixed,
that patience is a form of love,
that the things we build with care
outlast us.

Years later, she built something of her own.
And on the wall — the first thing they made together.

What we teach with our hands can live for generations.
```

### On-Screen Text
*"What we teach with our hands can live for generations."*

## Story 5 — The Wedding Day

**Format:** Short form 30–45 seconds / Medium form 90 seconds
**ICP:** ICP1 — emotional peak; resonates with protective parents of adult children

### Shot List
1. Childhood: a parent and young child dancing in the kitchen, no music needed.
2. Teenage years: a breakup — the child crying, the parent sitting beside them. No words. Just present.
3. College drop-off. The parent helps carry boxes. Hard hug at the curb.
4. The wedding day — getting ready. The parent adjusts the dress/suit jacket.
5. At the altar — the child looks back one more time.
6. They reach back and squeeze the parent's hand before stepping forward.
7. The parent, composed — barely.
8. Tree and two hands visual.

### Voiceover
```
They have always looked for you first.

When they were afraid.
When they were proud.
When the world felt too big.

And even on the day they walk into their own life —
even then,
the first hand they reach for is yours.

Being there is the gift they will remember.

Even when you're no longer standing beside them —
make sure your love is still in the room.
```

### On-Screen Text
*"Being there is the gift they remember."*

## Story 6 — The Final Expense Reflection
*(The Parent Who Prepared)*

**Format:** Short form 30 seconds — retargeting / ICP2 specific
**ICP:** ICP2 (Adult Child)

### Shot List
1. An adult child looking through an older parent's belongings — boxes, papers.
2. Old photos: the parent younger, the child smaller. Quiet nostalgia.
3. A simple folder labeled — not with fear, but with care. A plan.
4. The adult child sits back. Something settles in them.
5. Evermore tree close.

### Voiceover
```
The people we love should not have to guess what we wanted.
They should not have to carry confusion on top of grief.

Final expense coverage can be one way to create clarity.

Not because we expect the worst.
Because love prepares.
Because showing up doesn't stop when we do.
```

### On-Screen Text
*"Your family deserves clarity, not confusion."*

## Production Notes (All Stories)

- Each story shares the same **visual universe** as the Soccer Dad hero video: warm light, real homes, authentic faces, tree symbolism
- Character continuity is not required across stories — these are illustrative narrative types, not the same family
- Every story ends with the Evermore Life tree logo + one of the core taglines
- Short-form versions should run 30–45 seconds; extend to 90 seconds for medium form with more scene depth
- All stories should have captions — 85%+ of social video watched without sound
- Color grade, music, and voiceover should be consistent with the Soccer Dad hero video aesthetic
