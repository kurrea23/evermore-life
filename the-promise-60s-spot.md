# "The Promise" — Evermore Life 60-Second Spot

**Length:** 60 seconds, fast cuts (5–10s scenes)
**Platform:** Facebook (feed + Reels)
**Product:** Permanent life insurance / IUL — reads as legacy + living benefits, NOT final expense
**Target:** Working-age parents (30s–50s), healthy, planning ahead for their kids
**Emotional hook:** A dad teaches his son soccer his whole life and promises to always watch him play. He plans early so that promise — and his family — are protected no matter what. He keeps watching, even after he's gone.

## How this reads as IUL (not final expense)
- **Young, healthy dad** signing the policy in his 30s = permanent coverage you buy early and keep forever.
- **"Grew alongside his son" (Scene 4)** = cash value accumulation over time.
- **"Living benefits funded the dream" (Scene 5)** = accessing cash value while alive — the key IUL differentiator final-expense products don't have.
- **"A promise that outlives you" (end card)** = the death benefit as legacy, not just burial costs.
This keeps it aspirational and forward-looking, which is what speaks to the working-parent buyer.

## Production notes
- **Casting:** One dad, one boy aged up across the spot (cast 3–4 kids at different ages who resemble each other, or use the same family over a short pickup if possible).
- **Captions:** Burn in all on-screen text — Facebook plays muted by default.
- **Music:** One track that builds and resolves; carries the emotion since there's little/no dialogue after Scene 1.
- **Ratios:** Shoot 4K → export 9:16 (Reels), 1:1 (feed), 16:9 (landing page).
- **Shootability:** This is doable as a phone/DIY shoot or a half-day with a freelancer. The lift is casting believable kids and getting field + backyard locations.

## Compliance reminder
IUL/permanent life claims (cash value, living benefits, guarantees) are regulated. Before publishing, confirm wording against your state requirements (AZ, TX, pending AR) and add any required disclosures to the end card or the Facebook post copy.
