# Front-End Partnership Strategy

## Best-Fit Partners

For an IUL, retirement planning, family protection, and mortgage protection brand, prioritize partners who meet people during major financial decisions.

1. Mortgage brokers and loan officers
   - Natural mortgage-protection conversation after a home purchase or refinance.
   - Co-branded homeowner protection review.

2. Realtors and real-estate teams
   - New homeowners often need income and mortgage protection.
   - Provide a post-closing family protection checklist.

3. Estate-planning attorneys and will/trust providers
   - Strong fit for legacy, beneficiary, liquidity, and family-protection conversations.
   - Keep legal advice and insurance advice clearly separated.

4. CPAs, tax preparers, and enrolled agents
   - Good source of introductions to business owners and households planning long term.
   - Never imply a tax result without the client's tax professional confirming it.

5. Employee-benefits consultants and small-business owners
   - Opens conversations about supplemental coverage, executive benefits, key-person coverage, and retirement gaps.

6. Financial wellness educators and community organizations
   - Workshops can teach protection basics, beneficiary reviews, and retirement-income concepts without turning into a hard sell.

## Secondary Partners

- Property and casualty agents
- Divorce and family-law professionals
- Business brokers and succession advisers
- Credit unions and community banks, subject to compliance approval
- Retirement communities for annuity, legacy, and beneficiary reviews

## Lower-Priority Partners

Senior homes, funeral homes, and burial-focused businesses fit a final-expense lane more than this new primary brand. They can remain a separate referral channel later, but they should not drive the front-end identity of this website.

## Partnership Offer

Lead with a useful service rather than "send us leads":

- Free family protection review
- Homeowner mortgage-protection review
- Beneficiary and legacy checklist
- Retirement-income strategy review
- Educational workshop with a licensed insurance professional

Every partnership should have written consent, clear roles, compliant disclosures, and a review of referral-compensation rules before launch.
