Common Questions

You've Got Questions. We've Got Answers.

How much does final expense insurance cost? +
Cost depends on your age, health, and how much coverage you want. Many people in their 50s and 60s pay between $30–$70/month for $10,000–$20,000 in coverage. The best way to know your exact rate is to answer a few quick questions — Sarah can help you get started in under 2 minutes.
Do I need a medical exam to get covered? +
Many final expense plans do not require a medical exam. We ask a few simple health questions, and many health situations can still qualify for coverage. For harder-to-insure situations, guaranteed-issue options may be available.
What if I have health problems? Will I still qualify? +
Often, yes. We work with carriers that consider a range of health situations, including diabetes, COPD, heart conditions, and more. If standard plans are not available, guaranteed-issue options may still be worth reviewing.
I already have life insurance. Do I need this too? +
Final expense insurance is specifically designed for end-of-life costs. Many people with existing life insurance find their policy isn't enough to cover funeral costs PLUS ongoing family expenses. Final expense fills that specific gap — it's a targeted, affordable addition.
Will my rate ever go up? +
No. Your premium is locked in for life the day you're approved. It never increases due to age or health changes. That's one of the key benefits of final expense insurance — predictable, stable coverage.
How does the free consultation work? +
Just 15 minutes on the phone with one of our licensed Evermore Life agents. They'll review your answers from Sarah, compare carrier options, and show you exact rates and coverage amounts you qualify for. Zero pressure — if it's not right for you, no problem at all.
